---
title: Tracking Interactions
description: Log pitches, follow-ups, and outcomes so you know exactly where things stand with every industry contact.
---

Every contact in ReleaseLoop has an interaction timeline -- a running log of your communication history so you never lose track of what was pitched, what was promised, and what came of it.

## Logging an interaction

1. Open a contact's detail page
2. Scroll to the **Interactions** section
3. Click **Add Interaction**
4. Enter what happened -- the release you pitched, their response, any follow-up they requested, and next steps
5. The interaction is timestamped and added to the timeline

For example: you pitched a new single to a playlist curator, they asked to hear it after mastering is done, and you need to follow up next Friday. Log that now so it does not slip through the cracks.

## Interaction timeline

The timeline shows all logged interactions in chronological order, giving you a complete history of your relationship with each contact. This is where the real value builds up over time:

- **Before a pitch** -- check what you sent this curator last time, how long ago it was, and whether they placed the track
- **During a campaign** -- see at a glance which contacts have responded and which still need a follow-up
- **Across releases** -- know which bloggers have covered your artists before and which radio programmers have added your music to rotation

## Success tracking

ReleaseLoop tracks outcomes for each contact so you can identify the people who consistently deliver results:

- **Successful outcomes** -- the pitch led to a playlist placement, a blog feature, radio adds, a sync license, or press coverage
- **Pending outcomes** -- you are still waiting on a response or a decision

Over time, this gives you a clear picture of your most valuable contacts -- the curators who actually add tracks, the bloggers who follow through on reviews, and the sync supervisors who place music. Focus your energy on the relationships that move the needle.

## Notes

Each contact also has a free-form notes field for internal context that does not fit into the interaction timeline -- things like preferred pitch format, the best day to email them, how you originally connected, genres they have said they are not interested in, or a reminder that they only review tracks with press photos attached.
