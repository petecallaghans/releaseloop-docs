The **Marketing** tab on a release is where you plan and coordinate your entire promotional campaign. From pitching Spotify editorial playlists weeks before drop day to posting performance recaps after release -- every activity gets tracked in one place.

## Adding marketing activities

1. Open a release and go to the **Marketing** tab
2. Click **Add Activity**
3. Fill in:
   - **Title** -- what you are doing (e.g., "Pitch to Spotify editorial via Spotify for Artists," "Post 30-second teaser clip on TikTok," "Send album announcement to mailing list")
   - **Platform** -- where it will happen (Instagram, TikTok, Spotify, YouTube, Email, Press, etc.)
   - **Phase** -- when in the release cycle: Pre-save, Release Week, or Post-Release
   - **Status** -- Draft, Scheduled, Published, or Completed
   - **Date** -- when the activity is planned

## Release phases

Marketing activities are organized into three phases that mirror how release campaigns actually work:

| Phase | When | What you are typically doing |
|-------|------|------------------------------|
| **Pre-save** | 2-4 weeks before release day | Setting up pre-save links, posting teaser content on Instagram and TikTok, pitching Spotify editorial playlists (must be done at least 7 days before release), sending announcements to your mailing list, reaching out to press and blogs |
| **Release Week** | The week of release | Launch posts across all platforms, press release distribution, email blasts to fans, coordinating with the artist on Stories and live sessions, adding the track to your own playlists |
| **Post-Release** | After release day | Sharing streaming milestones, posting behind-the-scenes content, following up on playlist adds, running paid promotion if the track is gaining traction, planning remix or acoustic versions |

## Using with templates

You can include marketing activities in [release templates](/templates/release-templates/) to reuse your promotional playbook across releases. When you apply a template, both tasks and marketing activities are imported -- so your go-to rollout strategy is ready in seconds. This is especially valuable if you run a label releasing music regularly and want every campaign to hit the same baseline of promotional effort.

## Calendar integration

All marketing activities with dates appear on the [Calendar](/templates/calendar/) view, giving you a timeline of promotional efforts across all your releases. This is critical when you have multiple releases in the pipeline -- you can spot scheduling conflicts, avoid overlapping campaigns for artists on your roster, and make sure each release gets the attention it deserves.
