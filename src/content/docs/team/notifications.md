title: Notifications

When you are running a release campaign, missed deadlines cost you. A late playlist pitch means you miss the editorial window. A forgotten distributor delivery means your release does not go live on time. Notifications keep your team accountable.

## Notification types

| Notification | When it triggers |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **Task assigned** | When a task is assigned to you -- e.g., your label manager assigns you "Pitch to Spotify editorial" |
| **Task due soon** | 24 hours before a task's due date -- your last chance to get it done before the deadline |
| **Task overdue** | When a task passes its due date without being completed -- flags that something slipped |

## In-app notifications

Click the **bell icon** in the top navigation to view your notifications. New notifications appear as a badge count on the bell.

From the notification panel you can:

- View all recent notifications
- Click a notification to jump straight to the relevant task or release
- Mark notifications as read

## Email notifications

Critical notifications are also sent via email. This means your team gets alerted about approaching deadlines even when they are not actively in ReleaseLoop -- important for freelance publicists or part-time collaborators who are not in the app every day.

## Configuring preferences

1. Go to **Settings > Notifications**
2. Toggle each notification type on or off

By default, notifications are turned off. Enable the ones that matter to your workflow.

## Recommended setup

- **For label managers and artist managers:** Enable all notification types so you see when tasks are assigned, approaching, and overdue across your roster.
- **For A&R and marketing team members:** Enable "Task assigned" so you know immediately when you have new work, and "Task due soon" so you get a 24-hour heads-up before deadlines.
- **For everyone on a release campaign:** At minimum, turn on "Task due soon." A 24-hour warning before a playlist pitch deadline or a content due date can save an entire release cycle.
