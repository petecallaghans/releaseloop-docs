---
title: Electronic Press Kits (EPKs)
description: Create shareable EPK pages for booking agents, promoters, press, and sync supervisors.
---

An Electronic Press Kit (EPK) is a public-facing profile page for an artist that you can send to booking agents, promoters, venues, press outlets, sync supervisors, and anyone else who needs a professional overview of the act. Instead of assembling a one-off PDF or email with scattered links, you share a single URL that always has the latest info.

:::note
EPKs require a **Solo** plan or higher. The number of EPKs depends on your plan (5 on Solo, 15 on Team, 75 on Label, unlimited on Label Pro).
:::

## Creating an EPK

1. Go to an artist's detail page
2. Click the **EPK** tab
3. Toggle the EPK to **enabled**
4. Configure the sections and settings to match what you want the recipient to see

## EPK sections

An EPK can include:

- **Stats** -- Spotify followers, popularity, and other streaming metrics that booking agents and curators look for
- **Press photos** -- high-resolution images for media use, festival marketing, and venue promo
- **Videos** -- music videos, live performances, interviews, and session recordings
- **Accolades** -- awards, chart positions, notable press quotes, and sync placements
- **Quotes** -- pull quotes from reviews, interviews, or tastemaker endorsements
- **Social links** -- all the artist's social media profiles in one place
- **Discography** -- selected releases to showcase the artist's catalog
- **Contacts** -- booking, management, and PR contacts (controlled by your settings so you decide what is visible)

## EPK settings

Configure how the EPK appears and who can access it:

- **Visibility** -- Public (anyone with the link) or Private (hidden until you are ready to share)
- **Search indexing** -- Allow or prevent search engines from indexing the page -- keep it off if you only want to share directly with specific contacts
- **Theme** -- Dark, Light, or System (follows the visitor's preference)
- **Contact display** -- Choose which contact info appears on the public page so you can show booking details without exposing personal information

## Sharing your EPK

Once enabled, each EPK gets a unique public URL. Send it in pitch emails to promoters, include it in booking inquiry responses, or add it to the artist's link-in-bio. The page is standalone -- visitors do not need a ReleaseLoop account to view it.

## Tips for a strong EPK

- Keep press photos high-resolution so media outlets and festival organizers can download and use them directly
- Include recent accolades, chart positions, and press quotes -- these are often the first things a booking agent or curator scans for
- Use the contact settings to show booking and management emails without exposing personal phone numbers or addresses
- Review your EPK from the public link before sending it out to make sure the layout, photos, and content look right from the visitor's perspective
