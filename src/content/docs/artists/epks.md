---
title: Electronic Press Kits (EPKs)
description: How to create and manage public EPK pages for your artists.
---

An Electronic Press Kit (EPK) is a public-facing profile page for an artist that you can share with press, promoters, venues, and industry contacts.

:::note
EPKs require a **Solo** plan or higher. The number of EPKs depends on your plan (5 on Solo, 25 on Team, unlimited on Label).
:::

## Creating an EPK

1. Go to an artist's detail page
2. Click the **EPK** tab
3. Toggle the EPK to **enabled**
4. Configure the sections and settings

## EPK sections

An EPK can include:

- **Stats** -- Spotify followers, popularity, and other metrics
- **Press photos** -- professional images for media use
- **Videos** -- music videos, live performances, interviews
- **Accolades** -- awards, chart positions, press quotes
- **Quotes** -- pull quotes from reviews or interviews
- **Social links** -- all the artist's social media profiles
- **Discography** -- selected releases
- **Contacts** -- booking, management, and PR contacts (controlled by settings)

## EPK settings

Configure how the EPK appears and who can access it:

- **Visibility** -- Public (anyone with the link) or Private (hidden)
- **Search indexing** -- Allow or prevent search engines from indexing the page
- **Theme** -- Dark, Light, or System (follows visitor's preference)
- **Contact display** -- Choose which contact info appears on the public page

## Sharing your EPK

Once enabled, each EPK gets a unique public URL you can share. The page is standalone and doesn't require the visitor to have a ReleaseLoop account.

## Tips

- Keep press photos high-resolution for media download
- Include recent accolades and quotes to make a strong impression
- Use the contact settings to control what info is publicly visible
- Review your EPK from the public link to see exactly what visitors see
